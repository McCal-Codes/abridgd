**This Confidentiality & Non‑Disclosure Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of the date signed below between McCal Media LLC (d/b/a "Abridgd") and the undersigned Recipient (Tester).**

## 1. Purpose

The Disclosing Party will provide Recipient access to pre‑release software, builds, designs, documents, analytics, test data, and other information related to the mobile app “Abridgd” (the “Confidential Information”) for the sole purpose of beta testing and providing feedback.

## 2. Definition of Confidential Information

“Confidential Information” means non‑public information disclosed orally, visually, or in writing related to Abridgd, including unreleased builds, screenshots, features, roadmaps, code, business plans, test notes, logs, performance data, and any derivatives thereof. Confidential Information does not include information that:

- a) was publicly available at the time of disclosure or becomes publicly available through no breach by Recipient;
- b) was rightfully in Recipient’s possession prior to disclosure (and can be evidenced by written records);
- c) is received by Recipient from a third party without breach of any obligation of confidentiality; or
- d) is independently developed by Recipient without use of Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information.

## 3. Non‑Disclosure & Use

Recipient shall:

- a) keep Confidential Information strictly confidential and use at least the same degree of care as used to protect its own confidential information (no less than reasonable care);
- b) use Confidential Information solely for the Purpose;
- c) not copy, reproduce, publish, distribute or disclose Confidential Information to any third party except as permitted below; and
- d) not reverse engineer, decompile, or attempt to derive source code or algorithms from any provided build or binary.

## 4. Permitted Disclosures

Recipient may disclose Confidential Information only to its employees, contractors, or advisors who have a bona fide need to know for the Purpose and who are bound by confidentiality obligations no less protective than those in this Agreement. Recipient remains liable for any breach by such persons.

## 5. Return or Destruction

Upon Disclosing Party’s request or upon termination of Recipient’s access, Recipient will promptly return or destroy all Confidential Information (including copies) and certify in writing that it has done so, except that one archival copy may be retained in encrypted form for legal/compliance purposes.

Notwithstanding the foregoing, obligations with respect to trade secrets shall survive for as long as the information qualifies as a trade secret under applicable law.
