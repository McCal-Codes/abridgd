# Vision: Abridged

Last Updated: 2026-01-18

## The Problem

Local news and community coordination is fragmented. News is either overwhelming (infinite feeds), distracting (pop-ups/ads), or inaccessible (paywalls). Meanwhile, community resources and events are scattered across social media, bulletin boards, and word-of-mouth. People want to stay informed about their community, connect with local happenings, and access resources without the mental tax of modern web engagement or platform hopping.

## The Solution

**Abridged** is a unified platform for Pittsburgh's news, community, and resources—all in one calm, focused place. It's a daily companion for staying informed, getting involved, and accessing what matters locally.

### What Abridged Provides

1. **Curated News** — Daily digest of Pittsburgh stories from trusted local sources
2. **Community Events** — Protests, gatherings, town halls, and community meetings
3. **Local Resources** — Food banks, farmers markets, mutual aid, public services
4. **Calm Reading** — Two modes: Classic (traditional) and Abridged (RSVP speed reader)

### Core Values

1.  **Calm:** No breaking news banners, no red badges, no infinite scroll loops.
2.  **Finite:** There is a "done" state—you can catch up completely.
3.  **Focused:** Content is the primary action. Navigation is minimal and intentional.
4.  **Community-First:** Pittsburgh's people and their needs come before engagement metrics.
5.  **Accessible:** Information should be free, clear, and available to all.

### Target Audience

- **The Urban Professional:** Busy, cares about Pittsburgh, but doesn't have time to sift through clutter or multiple platforms.
- **The Intentional Reader:** Someone who prefers quality over quantity and wants to support local journalism.
- **The Commuter:** Needs quick catch-ups or deep dives depending on their schedule.
- **The Community Organizer:** Looking for a centralized way to discover events, resources, and causes.
- **The Engaged Citizen:** Wants to stay informed, attend events, and support local initiatives without platform fatigue.

## Community Features

Beyond news, Abridged serves as Pittsburgh's community hub:

### Events & Activism
- **Community Calendar:** Protests, marches, town halls, neighborhood meetings
- **Grassroots Organizing:** Connect with local movements and causes
- **Civic Engagement:** Stay informed about local government and policy

### Local Resources
- **Food Access:** Food banks, community fridges, farmers markets
- **Mutual Aid:** Community support networks and resource sharing
- **Public Services:** Library hours, rec centers, city resources
- **Small Business:** Support local shops, restaurants, and services

### Why This Matters
Pittsburgh's community information is currently scattered across Facebook groups, bulletin boards, Twitter threads, and neighborhood listservs. Abridged consolidates what matters into one calm, organized space—no algorithms deciding what you see, no engagement manipulation.

## RSVP (Rapid Serial Visual Presentation)

Abridged introduces a secondary reading mode based on RSVP. This technology flashes words one at a time at a user's controlled speed.

- **Why?** To allow users to ingest information quickly without eye movement fatigue.
- **When?** Specific "Abridged Mode" for commuting or quick scanning—perfect for reading news or event details on the bus.
- **Accessibility:** Great for users with dyslexia or other reading challenges.

## Emotional Safety & Grounding

News can be traumatic. Abridged implements thoughtful features for emotional well-being:

### Grounding Mode
*   **Consent First:** Sensitive or graphic content is never shown without explicit tap-through consent.
*   **Calming Alternatives:** Users can choose to "Ground Themselves" instead of reading. This displays a soothing experience (e.g., breathing exercise or calming visuals) to reset the nervous system.
*   **Take a Break:** Option to step away from news and explore community resources or positive local stories instead.

### Why This Matters
Local news often includes difficult topics—crime, tragedy, hardship. We believe staying informed shouldn't come at the cost of mental health. Users should have agency over their emotional exposure.

## Design Philosophy

### Information Hierarchy
1. **Content First:** Articles, events, resources are primary
2. **Navigation Second:** Minimal chrome, intentional pathways
3. **Actions Third:** Save, share, engage—available but not pushy

### Visual Language
- **Glass Morphism:** iOS 26-inspired blur effects create depth without distraction
- **Typography:** Clean, readable, serif for long-form content
- **Color:** Colorblind-friendly cyan accent, neutral backgrounds
- **Motion:** Subtle spring physics, haptic feedback for confidence
- **Dark Mode:** Full support for late-night reading without eye strain

### Interaction Patterns
- **Gestures:** Swipe right to go back, swipe left to save—natural, learnable
- **Haptics:** Tactile feedback confirms actions without interrupting flow
- **States:** Clear loading, empty, and error states—never leave users guessing

## Long-Term Vision

Abridged starts with Pittsburgh, but the model is replicable for any city:
- **Local-First:** Every city deserves a calm, community-focused information hub
- **No Surveillance:** No selling data, no tracking for ads, no algorithmic manipulation
- **Sustainable:** Support local journalism through partnerships, not extractive platforms
- **Open Standard:** Potentially open-source the framework for other cities to adapt

### Success Looks Like
- **For Readers:** "I check Abridged every morning—it's my daily Pittsburgh ritual."
- **For Organizers:** "We post our events to Abridged because that's where people actually look."
- **For Journalists:** "Abridged sends more engaged readers to our articles than social media ever did."
- **For the City:** "Abridged helped us become a more informed, connected, and resilient community."
