# Vision: Abridged

Last Updated: 2026-01-16

## The Problem

Local news consumption is broken. It's either overwhelming (infinite feeds), distracting (pop-ups/ads), or inaccessible (paywalls). Users want to stay informed about their community without the mental tax of modern web engagement.

## The Solution

**Abridged** is a calm, finite, and focused news reader for Pittsburgh. It respects the user's attention and time.

### Core Values

1.  **Calm:** No breaking news banners, no red badges, no infinite scroll loops.
2.  **Finite:** There is a "done" state.
3.  **Focused:** Reading is the primary action. Navigation is secondary.

### Target Audience

- **The Urban Professional:** Busy, cares about Pittsburgh, but doesn't have time to sift through clutter.
- **The Intentional Reader:** Someone who prefers quality over quantity.
- **The Commuter:** Needs quick catch-ups or deep dives depending on their schedule.

## RSVP (Rapid Serial Visual Presentation)

Abridged introduces a secondary reading mode based on RSVP. This technology flashes words one at a time at a user's controlled speed.

- **Why?** To allow users to ingest information quickly without eye movement fatigue.
- **When?** Specific "Abridged Mode" for commuting or quick scanning.

## Emotional Safety & Grounding
News can be traumatic. Abridged implements a "Grounding Mode" for sensitive or breaking news.
*   **Consent First:** Graphic content is never shown without explicit tap-through consent.
*   **Calming Alternatives:** Users can choose to "Ground Themselves" instead of reading. This displays a soothing, non-news experience (e.g., a breathing exercise or abstract pleasant imagery) to reset the nervous system.
